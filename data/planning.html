Expert rationale (how the insights shaped refinements) Tap to expand

Metrics → “Stabilize the new platform; stop the spike/monotony loop”

  • Your chronic baseline has climbed rapidly and is now the highest in the dataset, but achieved via surges that repeatedly triggered spike flags (high uncoupled ACWR + high ramp_7d). This plan keeps the same race-driven phase structure but strengthens the expectation of planned oscillation (build → consolidate) rather than heroic ramps.
  • Strain has recently been monotony-driven (monotony > 2.0) as much as volume-driven. The plan therefore treats weekly variability and true low-load days as a strategic requirement, not optional.
  • Because deloads quickly normalize spike indicators in your data, the plan intentionally uses consolidation periods (Phase 1, race tapers, post-race re-entry, and summer rebuild rhythm) as proactive resets.

Activity → “Double down on threshold + steady cycling; clean up ‘gray-zone’ running”

  • You execute cruise-interval threshold well (controlled pacing/HR/power), so the plan keeps threshold as the primary quality language across the whole arc.
  • Your frequent “steady rolling trail runs” behave like strength-endurance sessions (often moderate HR). The plan explicitly treats these as quality-ish, so they’re managed like a stimulus that needs spacing—especially near threshold or long durability work.
  • Cycling is a clear strength and a low-impact way to accumulate aerobic load with minimal drift; this plan elevates cycling as a key durability lever in the Adventure Race build.
  • Your sprinting is often “surges on a steady run.” Useful for trails, but not pure speed development—so the plan calls for clearer separation: surges for trail specificity, and rare pure neuromuscular touches only when fresh.

Physiology → “You rebound well, but sleep/stress stacking is the tripwire”

  • You show good resilience (HRV rebounds quickly; RHR trending down into March), so we can keep a 24-week arc with meaningful builds.
  • The main risk is stacking stressors (sleep fragmentation + high stress) before you rebound. The plan’s consolidation points are also physiology-protection points, and taper periods emphasize sleep and stress reduction as performance-critical.
  • Weight loss/under-fueling is flagged as a potential medium-term limiter; fueling practice is introduced early (Phase 3) and reinforced through the Adventure Race specificity block.
Constraints (qualitative guardrails) Tap to expand

Load & progression constraints (Metrics-driven)

  • No more “shock weeks” as a default tool: progression should be intentionally boring.
  • Spike detection priority: monitor uncoupled ACWR + ramp_7d + monotony; if all rise together, insert an immediate consolidation/downshift.
  • Monotony is a primary constraint: avoid weeks where every day looks the same effort/terrain; include real low-load days and varied modalities.

Training-intent constraints (Activity-driven)

  • Treat rolling trail steady runs as a stimulus: don’t stack them repeatedly with other quality.
  • Keep quality anchored in what you execute best: cruise-interval threshold and steady aerobic cycling.
  • Speed is “high quality, low quantity”: avoid turning speed into more aerobic grind via short recoveries when fatigue is present.
  • Strength stays supportive: prioritize maintaining durability without compromising the next key run quality.

Recovery/readiness constraints (Physiology-driven)

  • Readiness tripwire: multiple nights of HRV suppressed (≈ low-40s or below) + RHR up ~3–5 bpm above recent baseline + fragmented sleep/high stress → immediate load reduction and focus on sleep.
  • Avoid repeated hits: don’t add new intensity/volume until recovery markers and subjective freshness normalize.
  • Fueling is part of training: persistent weight loss or worsening recovery signals despite “normal” sleep time → treat as an under-recovery/under-fueling risk and adjust training stress accordingly.
