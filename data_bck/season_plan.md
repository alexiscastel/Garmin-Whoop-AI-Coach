# 3) Constraints (qualitative guardrails for this season)

## Load & progression constraints (from Metrics)
- **Avoid repeating spike blocks**: build should feel “boring and sustainable,” not heroic.
- **Ramp control:** keep most progression weeks in **single digits to low teens** for ramp-like behavior; reserve big jumps for rare, deliberate situations.
- **ACWR awareness:** treat **>2.0** as “spike territory” (use as a red flag); aim for more controlled ranges most of the time.
- **Monotony management:** design weekly variety so monotony doesn’t repeatedly exceed the high-risk band (your data shows >2.0 weeks produced extreme strain).

## Training-intent constraints (from Activity)
- **Separation of intent:** avoid living in the moderate-hard middle every day; protect truly easy days.
- **Long-run discipline:** long efforts are primarily aerobic builders; if they drift into high strain, adjust execution rather than “toughing it out.”
- **Quality preference:** prioritize threshold development + strides over frequent VO₂/anaerobic work (use sharpening sparingly).
- **Cross-training as strategy:** use steady cycling to add aerobic volume while limiting eccentric run load—especially when trails/hills increase musculoskeletal cost.

## Recovery / readiness constraints (from Physiology)
- **Watch the stress-stack cluster:** if you see multi-day patterns like **HRV staying low (≈41–42 range), RHR rising (≈49–50), higher stress avg, and poor sleep**, respond with immediate downshift (reduce strain, add recovery, protect sleep).
- **Fueling is a limiter:** continued weight loss plus suppressed HRV relative to baseline is a performance risk—treat fueling and sleep as part of the plan, not optional.
- **Tapers are for freshness, not fitness gains:** especially before the **May 30, 2026 A-race**.
