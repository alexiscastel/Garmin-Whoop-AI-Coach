✅ Recommendations (brief, actionable)

🧩 A) Next 7 days (consolidate the high-strain week)

  1. Reduce monotony immediately: aim for at least 2 truly easy/low-load days this week (or 1 easy + 1 rest), instead of stacking medium days. This is the fastest way to drop strain while preserving chronic gains.
  2. Keep one quality workout, not two: repeat a threshold-style session similar to 03-04 (controlled reps, consistent output), but keep total quality volume modest.
  3. Long run stays, but cap effort: keep it easy on climbs (let pace slow), so it becomes “durability” rather than another steady/tempo day.

🧊 B) “Make easy easy” (the biggest ROI change)

  1. Use your treadmill easy sessions as an effort reference: if outdoor runs consistently land near HR ~150–156 and feel “steady,” deliberately back off so easy days feel like RPE 2–3/10. This will:
    • lower monotony,
    • improve readiness for threshold sessions,
    • and often helps VO2/device estimates recover.

⚡ C) Strides/sprints: choose the intent and structure it accordingly

  1. If the goal is neuromuscular speed, keep the surges short but make recoveries very easy (walk/jog) so HR can drop and the surges are truly snappy.
  2. If the goal is aerobic strength with spice, keep your current format—but then protect the next day as a genuine easy day.

🧭 D) Monitoring rules (simple decision support)

  1. If you see HRV below your balanced band and RHR elevated versus your recent lows for 2–3 consecutive days, respond with a 24–48 h reduction (easy/rest). You’ve shown that small reductions produce big readiness rebounds.
  2. If weekly monotony trends toward ~2+ again, add variation by inserting either:
    • one very low day, or
    • one higher day paired with a very low day (instead of multiple mediums).

🛠️ E) Data quality upgrades (to sharpen execution)

  1. Confirm HR reliability (sensor fit, battery, placement; consider a chest strap for key workouts). Given the anomalies, avoid letting a single HR reading override RPE/power/pace.
  2. Fix hydration logging (currently unusable). For trail half + adventure race prep, hydration and fueling practice are performance-critical—logging makes patterns visible.
