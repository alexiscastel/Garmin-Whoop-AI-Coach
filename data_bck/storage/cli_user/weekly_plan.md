## 1) Zones Table (use as *guardrails*; confirm on flat route / consistent treadmill)
**Key execution rule from your data:** keep *easy* truly easy (avoid drifting into the steady/hilly band unless the day calls for it).

### Run zones (power + RPE + typical pace anchors)
| Zone | Name | Power (heuristic) | RPE / talk test | Typical pace anchors (flat / treadmill) |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| Z1 | Recovery | **<275 W** | RPE 1–2; nose-breathing / full sentences | very easy; often **>6:30–6:40/km** |
| Z2 | Easy aerobic | **275–320 W** | RPE 2–3; conversational | ~**6:30–5:55/km** (your treadmill “easy” band) |
| Z3 | Steady / hills | **320–375 W** | RPE 3–4; can speak short sentences | ~**5:55–5:30/km** (varies with hills) |
| Z4 | Threshold | **385–415 W** | RPE 6–7; 1–3 words; repeatable | ~**4:55–4:45/km** (your best-executed reps) |
| Z5 | Strides / fast | **480–650 W** *(10–20s only)* | RPE 8–9; relaxed-fast mechanics | pace not the target; **form + pop** |

**Hill rule:** on non‑quality days, let pace slow and **cap climbs ~top of Z3** to prevent “moderate-hard middle” drift.

### Bike zones (RPE-based, since HR can be noisy)
| Zone | Name | RPE |
|---|---|---|
| BZ1 | Easy spin | 1–2 |
| BZ2 | Endurance | 2–3 |
| BZ3 | Tempo | 4–5 |

**Notation:** `r` = easy jog/walk (Z1–Z2) recovery.

### Readiness downshift rule (use daily)
- If you hit a **red** pattern (e.g., **HRV ≤ ~42–43** for 2 nights and/or **RHR ≥49–50**, poor sleep, high stress avg): **remove intensity first**, then cut duration 20–40%.
