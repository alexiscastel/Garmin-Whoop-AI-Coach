Phase 1 — Consolidate & De‑spike (Stabilization) Dates: Mar 9 → Mar 15, 2026 (1 week) Stabilize

Goal: Convert the recent load spike into fitness by reducing strain drivers (monotony/stacked stress) without losing frequency.

Themes:

  • Absorb + normalize: keep movement consistent, but avoid another “spike block.”
  • Reduce monotony: deliberately vary session types/terrain/easy days.
  • Recovery infrastructure: prioritize sleep regularity + fueling to support adaptation.

Why it matters now: Metrics show a very high recent acute load with high monotony/strain (“fit but fragile”). This week is the cap that prevents the next 10–14 days from becoming forced downtime.

Phase 2 — 10k Tune‑up (Controlled sharpening; minimal taper) Dates: Mar 16 → Mar 28, 2026 (2 weeks) → C‑race on Mar 28 Sharpen

Goal: Arrive at the 10k feeling snappy and confident, without sacrificing the bigger Half + Adventure Race build.

Themes:

  • Keep intensity controlled: threshold-style work and strides rather than deep anaerobic sessions.
  • Trail readiness: maintain hilly steady running for durability.
  • No hero taper: this is a C race—treat as a supported hard effort / benchmark.

Race use: Calibration point (execution + pacing discipline on trails), not a season peak.

Phase 3 — Trail Half Marathon Build (Stamina + trail durability) Dates: Mar 29 → Apr 18, 2026 (3 weeks) → B‑race on Apr 18 Build

Goal: Build sustainable threshold stamina and trail-specific strength to hit the Half confidently while still building toward the A‑priority Adventure Race.

Themes:

  • Threshold repeatability: cruise/threshold reps as the cornerstone.
  • Long-run discipline: long efforts stay aerobic and controlled (avoid “threshold-by-accident”).
  • Fueling practice begins: practice race fueling during longer sessions.

Race use: B‑race = light, controlled taper (fresh enough to perform; not so deep it disrupts the May build).

Phase 4 — Adventure Race Build 1 (Aerobic capacity + mixed-mode durability) Dates: Apr 19 → May 9, 2026 (3 weeks) Durability

Goal: Shift from run performance to 8‑hour adventure performance: durable aerobic output, low volatility pacing, tolerance for long time‑on‑feet.

Themes:

  • Volume with lower eccentric cost: expand aerobic load using steady cycling as a major lever.
  • Back-to-back durability: introduce weekend structure mimicking multi‑hour fatigue.
  • Terrain specificity: steady hilly work to build muscular endurance.
Phase 5 — Adventure Race Build 2 (Specificity + execution rehearsals) Dates: May 10 → May 16, 2026 (1 week) Rehearse

Goal: Convert fitness into race‑ready execution.

Themes:

  • Race-simulation emphasis: longer steady sessions rehearse pacing discipline and fueling.
  • Logistics + systems: gear checks, hydration plan, pacing guardrails.
  • Protect freshness: specificity increases, avoid stacking multiple strain-heavy days.
Phase 6 — Peak & Taper (A-race: 8‑hour Adventure Race) Dates: May 17 → May 30, 2026 (2 weeks) → A‑race on May 30 Peak

Goal: Arrive with high readiness (fresh legs + stable physiology) while maintaining “engine feel.”

Themes:

  • Reduce load, keep touch: maintain intensity feel via controlled efforts/strides.
  • Maximize recovery inputs: sleep consistency and stress down‑regulation become training.
  • No last-minute volume chasing: fitness is built; protect it.
Phase 7 — Post‑A Recovery + Re‑entry (includes C 10‑mile) Dates: May 31 → Jun 20, 2026 (3 weeks) → C‑race on Jun 13 Recover

Expert rationale (how insights shape the map) Expand

Metrics → “Smoother load architecture + planned downshifts”

  • High baseline + fast rebuild, but via spike blocks (very negative TSB, high ACWR, high monotony/strain). Plan starts with a stabilization week and repeats a build → consolidate rhythm.
  • Surround Apr 18 (B), May 30 (A), Aug 8 (B) with intentional load release rather than reactive rest.
  • Very high acute weeks (~800 acute_7d_sum) are occasional peaks near adventure specificity—not a default weekly target.

Activity → “Threshold repeatability, strides, hills, cycling; cleaner intent separation”

  • Default quality language: controlled threshold reps + steady aerobic runs with strides.
  • Trail/adventure specificity: hilly steady durability (explicitly guarded from becoming “hard days in disguise”).
  • Steady cycling used as an aerobic volume lever with lower eccentric cost—especially during adventure build phases.

Physiology → “Recovery infrastructure + stress stacking avoidance”

  • Moderate‑to‑high robustness, but sympathetic dominance episodes resolve with better sleep → frequent consolidations and strong taper recovery inputs.
  • Body‑mass decrease + HRV dip treated as a flag: plan assumes fueling and recovery are non‑negotiable for adaptation efficiency across 24 weeks.
Constraints (guardrails for the season) Expand

Load & progression (from Metrics)

  • Avoid repeating spike blocks: build should feel “boring and sustainable,” not heroic.
  • Ramp control: most progression weeks in single digits to low teens; reserve big jumps for rare, deliberate situations.
  • ACWR awareness: treat >2.0 as spike territory (red flag); aim for controlled ranges most of the time.
  • Monotony management: weekly variety so monotony doesn’t repeatedly exceed high‑risk band (>2.0 produced extreme strain historically).

Training intent (from Activity)

  • Separation of intent: don’t live in the moderate‑hard middle; protect truly easy days.
  • Long-run discipline: primarily aerobic builders—adjust execution rather than “toughing it out.”
  • Quality preference: threshold + strides over frequent VO₂/anaerobic work (use sharpening sparingly).
  • Cross-training as strategy: steady cycling to add aerobic volume while limiting run eccentric load.

Recovery/readiness (from Physiology)

  • Watch for clusters: HRV low (~41–42), RHR up (~49–50), higher stress avg, poor sleep → immediate downshift.
  • Fueling is a limiter: continued weight loss + suppressed HRV is a performance risk.
  • Tapers are for freshness, not fitness gains—especially before May 30 A‑race.
How to interpret races (inside the strategy) Expand
  • C races (Mar 28, Jun 13): minimal disruption; feedback + supported hard day.
  • B races (Apr 18, Aug 8): moderate taper; perform well while preserving the next build.
  • A race (May 30): full focus; taper + recovery are integral to performance outcome.
